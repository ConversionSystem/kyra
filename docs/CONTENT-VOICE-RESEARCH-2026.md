## Cross-platform implications for Kyra content

1. **LinkedIn is a dwell-time game.** Carousels beat text posts by 3–5× on engagement. Every daily LinkedIn draft should lead with carousel format unless the idea genuinely works better as text.

2. **Facebook wants conversations.** Every Facebook post should include a question designed to provoke reply chains, not hearts. The "parent post + threaded replies" structure is the single biggest reach multiplier.

3. **X suppresses links and rewards replies.** Every X post should be a thread, with no links in the main tweet, and the author should be ready to reply for the first 2–3 hours.

4. **Blog posts should target AI citations first, Google second.** Key takeaway boxes, FAQ sections, comparison tables, and freshness stamps drive GEO. Traditional SEO still matters but Google rankings no longer guarantee AI visibility.

5. **Private sharing is the hidden signal.** On Facebook especially, content that people DM/WhatsApp to friends gets massive reach boost. Design posts so they feel forward-worthy — specific, contrarian, or surprisingly useful.
